# Olist-Ecommerce-SQL-Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing the **Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist**. The analysis involves writing advanced SQL queries to solve critical business problems, extract data insights, and understand customer behavior, revenue streams, and product performance.

---

## 📊 Key Business Insights Extracted

### 1. Payment Method Preference & Revenue Analysis
* **Objective:** To identify which payment channels generate the most revenue and transactions.
* **Insight:** Customers heavily prefer **Credit Cards**, which drive both the highest transaction counts and total revenue for the platform.

### 2. Top Best-Selling Product Categories
* **Objective:** To determine the highest-performing product categories based on total units sold.
* **Insight:** Identified the Top 10 categories (such as bed_bath_table, health_beauty), helping the business optimize inventory and logistics.

### 3. Customer Density by City & State
* **Objective:** To locate where the maximum number of customers reside for targeted marketing.
* **Insight:** **São Paulo** ranks #1 in customer density, followed by **Rio de Janeiro**, highlighting the core geographic markets.

### 4. Customer Satisfaction Analysis
* **Objective:** To analyze the distribution of review scores (1 to 5 stars).
* **Insight:** The dataset shows high customer satisfaction, with **5-Star reviews** making up the largest portion of total feedback.

### 5. Monthly & Yearly Order Trends
* **Objective:** To track sales volume month-over-month to discover peak seasons and business growth over time.

---

## 🛠️ SQL Skills Applied
* **Data Aggregation:** `COUNT()`, `SUM()`, `AVG()`
* **Data Filtering & Sorting:** `GROUP BY`, `ORDER BY (ASC/DESC)`, `LIMIT`
* **Table Joins:** Combining customer, order, and product datasets using `JOIN ... ON`.
* **Data Cleaning:** Handling date/time strings using text-parsing and format handling (`STR_TO_DATE`, `SUBSTRING_INDEX`).

---

## 💾 Dataset Source
The data used in this project is a public dataset provided by Olist and can be found on Kaggle.
